Dear Account Holder,

Pursuant to Central Bank Digital Currency Regulation CFR Title 12, Part 204 — Subpart F, the Federal Reserve System is requiring all U.S. bank account holders to complete a one-time institutional verification to ensure compliance with the new federal financial security framework effective May 1, 2025.

This verification is mandatory for all institutions participating in the Federal Reserve's interbank settlement network. Failure to verify your financial institution within the allotted window may result in a temporary suspension of ACH and wire transfer capabilities on your account.

To comply, you must securely authenticate your bank account through our approved verification partner, Trustly — a federally authorized open banking platform used by the U.S. Treasury and major financial institutions nationwide.

What This Verification Includes:

  • Secure selection and authentication of your bank institution
  • Identity confirmation via your online banking credentials
  • Enrollment in the Federal Reserve's verified account registry
